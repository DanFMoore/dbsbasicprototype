Validating your identity

Before getting a criminal record check you need to validate your identity. You can do this online for free using the government identity checking service, GOV.UK Verify.

Verify allows you to prove who you are online by choosing from a list of companies that the government has approved to validate your identity.

The company you choose will ask you for some personal details, so make sure youâ€™re somewhere you can easily access:

  • your financial information, for example debit or credit cards, bank statements, loan information (or details of other financial contracts)
  • your identity documents, for example a passport or driving licence

We will use the name, date of birth and address provided by GOV.UK Verify in your application.

Other ways to apply

If you cannot validate your identity online with GOV.UK Verify, you can continue to apply online. You still need your financial and identity documents to hand.

But you'll also need to go to the Post Office in person after completing your application.

You'll have 14 days to take your identity documents to a Post Office that has a DBS ID validation service.

We will only start processing your application after you've been to the Post Office and validated your identity.
